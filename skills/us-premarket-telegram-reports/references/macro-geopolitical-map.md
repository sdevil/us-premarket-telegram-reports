# Macro and geopolitical impact map

Use this as the top-level regime filter before ranking individual long candidates.

## How to use

1. Assess whether a macro / geopolitical event is active.
2. Decide whether it primarily affects:
   - overall market risk appetite
   - a sector or theme
   - a specific company
3. Adjust:
   - market suitability
n- risk level
   - sector ranking
   - confidence in long setups
4. Do not let a strong single-stock story fully override a major macro shock without price/volume confirmation.

## Event buckets

### Middle East escalation / war shock / Iran-related conflict
Primary transmission:
- oil price
- shipping / transport risk
- defense spending expectations
- inflation expectations
- VIX / risk-off tone

Usually helps:
- energy
- defense
- selected commodity-linked names

Usually hurts or pressures:
- airlines
- transport
- consumer discretionary sensitivity
- high-duration growth if rates/inflation expectations rise

Prompt behavior:
- raise trading risk when shock is active
- lower willingness to chase fragile long breakouts
- explicitly ask whether sector tailwind or headwind overrides the stock-level catalyst

### Tariff / trade escalation / China policy shock
Primary transmission:
- industrial exposure
- China revenue exposure
- semiconductor export sensitivity
- supply chain uncertainty

Prompt behavior:
- re-rank sectors by tariff sensitivity
- downgrade names with direct policy exposure if thesis does not compensate for the risk
- do not assume a general market rally neutralizes trade-policy risk

### Fed / rates / inflation shock
Primary transmission:
- valuation compression / expansion
- duration sensitivity
- banks / real estate / software / unprofitable growth behavior

Prompt behavior:
- adjust market suitability and risk rating
- require stronger triggers for high-multiple growth names in hawkish conditions

### Oil spike without full war scenario
Primary transmission:
- energy outperformance
- inflation sensitivity
- airline / transport / consumer margin pressure

Prompt behavior:
- ask whether broad long setups are facing a macro tax from energy/inflation pressure

### Broad risk-off geopolitical event
Examples:
- surprise strike
- sanctions shock
- political instability
- major sovereign escalation

Prompt behavior:
- favor cleaner and more liquid setups only
- reduce confidence in lower-quality #3 ideas
- tighten breakout standards

## Review behavior

Post-market review should ask:
- Did macro/geopolitical context explain the difference between forecast and reality?
- Was the stock-level thesis correct but overruled by the regime?
- Should a future similar event automatically raise risk / lower conviction / change sector preference?
